# AI or Child?

One was written by a 5-year-old. The other by GPT-4. Good luck.

A quiz game where you read a sentence and guess whether it was said by a young child or generated by an AI. The twist: it's surprisingly hard to tell them apart.

## How to Play

1. A sentence appears on screen
2. Decide: was it said by a **child** (ages 3-7) or written by an **AI** (GPT-4, Claude, etc.)?
3. Click your answer or press **A** for AI, **C** for Child
4. See the reveal with context about who actually said it
5. Play through 20 rounds and get your rating

## Ratings

| Score | Title                       |
| ----- | --------------------------- |
| 18-20 | Turing Test Administrator   |
| 14-17 | Pretty Good Pattern Matcher |
| 10-13 | It's Harder Than It Looks   |
| 0-9   | Maybe You're the AI         |

## About the Dataset

50 curated entries, evenly split between real kid quotes and AI-generated text.

**Child quotes** are the kind of things kids actually say: confident wrong explanations, accidental profundity, bizarre logic, and creative descriptions of the world. Kids say things like "spaghetti is just tired noodles" with complete sincerity.

**AI quotes** are real outputs from language models asked to explain everyday things. They tend to be technically correct but subtly off - overly formal for casual topics, weirdly precise, or structured in a way no human would naturally speak.

The key design goal: make it genuinely hard. Some AI outputs sound surprisingly childlike. Some kid quotes are shockingly articulate.
